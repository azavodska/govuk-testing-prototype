Compliance metrics

Accessibility

  1. Meets level AA of WCAG 2.1

    Fully compliant
    Expert evaluation: An accessibility specialist (internal or external) has manually tested against WCAG and determined that it meets level AA of the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG 2.1) as a minimum

    Partially compliant
    Internal evaluation: Someone on the team with limited accessibility knowledge has performed some checks

    Not compliant
    Automated evaluation or no evaluation: Just an automated tool has been run, or no evaluation has been done

  2. Has an accurate accessibility statement

    Fully compliant
    Has a published accurate and up-to-date accessibility statement that explains how accessible the service is

    Partially compliant
    Has an partially complete or or outdated accessibility statement

    Not compliant
    Has no accessibility statement, or an inaccurate accessibility statement

  3. Works on most commonly used assistive technology

    Fully compliant
    Tested with, and is working on, all of GDS service standard listed access technologies and browsers. GOV.UK: Testing with assistive technologies

    Partially compliant
    Tested with a limited number of technologies and browsers, or has been tested but is not yet fully working

    Not compliant
    Tested with non/unknown assistive technologies, or not tested with any assistive technologies

  4. Disabled people are included in research

    Fully compliant
    1 in 5 research participants have an access need

    Partially compliant
    Some research conducted with users with access needs, but this is not yet regular practice

    Not compliant
    No research conducted with users with access needs

  5. Has plans to resolve key accessibility issues

    Fully compliant
    No accessibility issues present, or accessibility issues scheduled in a backlog with detailed acceptance criteria and a planned release date

    Partially compliant
    Accessibility issues have been identified and added to a team backlog, but not fully detailed or scheduled yet

    Not compliant
    Accesibility issues are not being addressed by the team

Inclusion

  1. Diverse user groups included in research

    Fully compliant
    1 in 5 research participants are from a group we know less about

    Partially compliant
    Some research conducted with users from groups we know less about, but this is not yet regular practice

    Not compliant
    No research conducted with users with groups we know less about

  2. Regular end-to-end usability testing

    Fully compliant
    Evidence that the team conducts regular end-to-end usability testing with a range of users

    Partially compliant
    Some ad hoc end-to-end usability testing done with users

    Not compliant
    No regular end-to-end usability testing done with users

  3. Exclusion mapping completed

    Fully compliant
    Exclusion mapping activity carried out with the full team and outputs translated into tickets on the backlog to improve inclusivity of the service

    Partially compliant
    Exclusion mapping exercise planned, but not completed yet

    Not compliant
    No exclusion mapping exercise undertaken

Standards

If services are compliant with the metrics above, they will be in line with the regulations and standards detailed here.

Public sector regulations (legal requirement)

  • Meet the Public Sector Bodies (Websites and Mobile Applications) (No. 2) Accessibility Regulations 2018

    Meet WCAG 2.1 to level AA (except where there are exceptions or disproportionate burden). This is in line with current NHS Digital policy: What all NHS services need to do about accessibility

  • Have an accessibility statement that complies with requirements GOV.UK: Guidance - Make your website or app accessible and publish an accessibility statement

GOV and NHS accessibility Standard

  • Meet WCAG 2.1 to level AA (except where there are exceptions or disproportionate burden)

  • Have an accessibility statement that complies with requirements

  • Has been tested and is compatible with specific AT and browser combinations GOV.UK: Technology - Testing with assistive technologies

  • User with a range of access needs have been included in User Research sessions in the design and testing of the service
