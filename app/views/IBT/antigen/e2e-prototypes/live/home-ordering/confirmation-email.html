What to do when you receive your test kit

  1. Read the instructions carefully. There is also guidance online and in other formats such as Easy Read: https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/testing-for-coronavirus-at-home
  2. Register your kit online, using your order ID at https://gov.uk/register-home-test
  3. Take the test. Here is a helpful video: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zCqo7MhQT6U
  4. Follow the instructions on how to return the kit
Royal Mail will be collecting from postboxes at different times over the Christmas period. Check the Royal Mail website for your nearest priority postbox and their collection times: https://www.royalmail.com/priority-postboxes

If you have symptoms, self-isolate straight away until you get your test results. Do not leave home, except to post your test kit. Check if people you live with need to self-isolate by going to https://www.nhs.uk/coronavirus and reading ‘Self-isolation and treating symptoms’.

Your kit may be different to the one in the video. Make sure you follow the instructions in your kit.

Read the test kit instructions for how to test people of different ages.

Get help to take the test

To have a live video call with trained staff download the free Be My Eyes app and go to the 'specialised help' section.

Instructions in alternative formats

Royal National Institute of Blind People (RNIB) can send you braille, audio or large print instructions.Order online at: https://www.rnib.org.uk/covidtest or call 0303 123 9999 (Monday to Friday 8am to 8pm and Saturday 9am to 1pm).

If you're worried

If at any stage over the next few days, you or someone you live with gets worse, or your symptoms do not get better after 7 days, use the online 111 coronavirus service https://111.nhs.uk/COVID-19. Only call 111 if you cannot get help online. In a medical emergency call 999.

Call 111 if you're worried about a baby or child. Call 999 if the baby or child seems very unwell, is getting worse, or you think there's something seriously wrong.

Test results

We'll send your test results to you by text and email. If you have symptoms, you must self-isolate until you get your results. If you do not have symptoms, follow the latest government guidance at https://www.gov.uk/coronavirus until you get your results.

If you test positive for coronavirus, we'll call you to find out who you may have come into contact with. We'll then contact them confidentially, to tell them to self-isolate.

If you have the NHS COVID-19 app and agree for it to anonymously alert anyone you've been in close contact with, the app will also tell them to self-isolate. To find out more visit https://covid19.nhs.uk

Antibody testing

If you agreed to do antibody testing, you will receive your first antibody test kit in the post 2 to 3 days after you get a positive PCR test result. Your 2nd antibody test kit will arrive 3 weeks after the first one.

The kit provides clear instructions on how to do the test. It’s important you do the test and post it back as soon as possible. You can leave home to post a test kit whilst self-isolating. Find more info on how to prepare for, take and return the antibody test: https://www.gov.uk/guidance/coronavirus-antibody-home-test-kit-taking-a-blood-sample

Getting help

If you need help, call 119 (free from mobiles and landlines). Lines are open 7am to 11pm.
