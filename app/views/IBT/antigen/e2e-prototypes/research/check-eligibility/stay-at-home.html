Based on your answers, you should be self-isolating. Self-isolation begins on the day symptoms start and continues for the next 10 full days.

Self-isolation is when you don't leave your home because you have or might have coronavirus.

This helps stop the virus speading to other people.

Self-isolation is different to:

  • social distancing — advice for everyone to avoid close contact with other people
  • shielding — advice for people at high risk from coronavirus

How to self-isolate

You must not leave your home if you are self-isolating.

Don't:

  • go to work, school or any public places - work from home if you can
  • go out to get food and medicine – order it online or by phone, or ask someone to bring it to your home
  • have visitors in your home, including friends and family – except for people providing essential care
  • go on public transport or use taxis
  • go out to exercise – exercise at home or in your garden, if you have one

There's support available to help you self-isolate.
