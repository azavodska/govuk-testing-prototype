Antibody testing

If you test positive for COVID-19, and you agree to antibody testing, we may send you antibody tests.

Antibody tests help the NHS learn how likely people are to get COVID-19 a second time or after a vaccination, and the impact of variants.

Not everyone who tests positive for COVID-19 will be sent antibody tests because there is a limited number. If you are sent antibody tests, you will:

  • get a text telling you an antibody test is on its way
  • be posted 2 antibody tests 3 weeks apart to your home address
  • do a fingerprick blood test on yourself at home each time
  • need to post back the completed antibody tests
  • get the test results sent to you
Video: How to collect your blood sample

! Warning By selecting Yes you confirm that you are OK doing a fingerprick blood test, and do not take anticoagulants ('blood-thinning' medicines) or have a blood clotting disorder such as haemophilia.
