What happens next

! Warning If you have a high temperature, a new continuous cough, or loss or change to your sense of smell or taste, you need to self-isolate immediately. If you have any other symptoms, you do not have to self-isolate until you get a positive result.
Check if people you live with need to self-isolate on NHS.UK

Your coronavirus home test kit should arrive in the next 2 days.

The test kit contains a swab, instructions on how to use it and return packaging.

You'll receive a confirmation email soon.

! Warning You no longer need to register your test kit. It has already been registered for you.

What to do when you receive your test kit

Follow the instructions in your test kit. The instructions will tell you:

  • how and when to do the swab test
  • how to return your sample

How to take the swab test

Watch a short video: how to take a swab test

Antibody testing

If you agreed to do antibody testing, you may receive an antibody test kit in the post, 2-3 days after you get a positive PCR test result. Your 2nd antibody test kit will arrive 3 weeks after the first one.

The kit contains clear instructions on how to do the test. It's important you do the test and post it back as soon as possible. You can leave home to post a test kit while self isolating. Find more info on how to prepare for, take and return the antibody test.

Instructions in alternative formats

Alternative format instructions are available online.

Royal National Institute of Blind People (RNIB) can send you braille, audio or large print instructions. Order online or call 0303 123 9999 (Monday to Friday 8am to 8pm and Saturday 9am to 1pm).

If you're worried

If your condition gets worse, or you do not get better after 7 days, use the NHS 111 online coronavirus service.

Only call 111 if you cannot get help online. For a medical emergency, dial 999.
