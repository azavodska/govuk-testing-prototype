An antibody test is a fingerprick blood test to check if you've have coronavirus (COVID-19) before.

You can do the test at home by yourself, you can ask a friend or relative to help if you want.

The fingerprick test lets us see if your current symptoms are from a reinfection.

Taking the test helps the NHS and research scientists learn more about who's had the virus more than once, and how it has spread.

You are only eligible for an antibody test if you test positive for coronavirus. The antibody test kits will be sent to you automatically if you test positive for coronavirus, we just need your delivery address.

There are a few things you need to do to prepare before doing your test, like drinking lots of water, soaking your hand in warm water and exercising if safe to do so.

Next, you'll use a lancet to prick the corner of your fingertip. It's only a small prick and you won't see any of the needle.

You'll then massage your hand, and fill your tube to the 600 line. Then you'll put the cap on and slowly turn the tube upside down.

Finally, you'll label your tube, package everything up and pop it in a postbox.
