Would {{ data['first-name'] or 'Pete' }} {{ data['last-name'] or 'Smith' }} also be willing to take a separate antibody test?

The antibody test will help the NHS learn more about who's had the virus more than once, and how it has spread.

What they need to know

  • They will only be sent an antibody test if they test positive for coronavirus.
  • They will receive 2 antibody tests in the post 3 weeks apart to their home address.
  • The antibody test is a ‘fingerprick’ blood test.
  • They do the test on themselves at home.
  • The completed antibody tests have to be posted back. They can leave home to post a test kit while self-isolating.
Video: How to collect your blood sample
Video transcript: What is a fingerprick test

There are a few things you need to do to prepare before doing your test, like drinking lots of water, soaking your hand in warm water and exercising if safe to do so.

Next, you'll use a lancet to prick the corner of your fingertip. It's only a small prick and you won't see any of the needle.

You'll then massage your hand, and fill your tube until the 600 line. Then you'll put the cap on and slowly turn the tube upside down.

Finally, you'll label your tube, package everything up and pop it in a postbox.

! Warning By selecting Yes you confirm that you are OK doing a fingerprick blood test, and do not take anticoagulants ('blood-thinning' medicines) or have a blood clotting disorder such as haemophilia.
