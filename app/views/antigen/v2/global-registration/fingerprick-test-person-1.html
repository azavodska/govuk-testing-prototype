Would {{ data['first-name-person-1'] or 'Jane' }} {{ data['last-name-person-1'] or 'Wilson' }} also be willing to take a separate antibody test?

They will only receive an antibody test kit if they test positive for coronavirus.

The antibody test is a 'fingerprick' blood test to see if you've had a previous infection. This will help the NHS learn more about who's had the virus more than once, and how it has spread.

They will be sent 2 antibody test kits. The kits will arrive by post at their home address 3 weeks apart. They do each test on themselves at home. They can ask a friend or relative to help.

The antibody tests have to be completed and posted back straight away. It's safe to leave home to post a test while you are self-isolating.


Video: How to collect your blood sample
Video transcript: What is a fingerprick test

There are a few things you need to do to prepare before doing your test, like drinking lots of water, soaking your hand in warm water and exercising if safe to do so.

Next, you'll use a lancet to prick the corner of your fingertip. It's only a small prick and you won't see any of the needle.

You'll then massage your hand, and fill your tube to the 600 line. Then you'll put the cap on and slowly turn the tube upside down.

Finally, you'll label your tube, package everything up and pop it in a postbox.

! Warning By selecting Yes you confirm that you are OK doing a fingerprick blood test, and do not take anticoagulants ('blood-thinning' medicines) or have a blood clotting disorder such as haemophilia.
